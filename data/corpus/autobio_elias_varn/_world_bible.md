## Geography

### The Continent of Aldara

A temperate landmass roughly the size of Western Europe, dominated by rolling hills, river valleys, and coastal plains. The climate ranges from Mediterranean in the south to cool maritime in the north.

### City-States (Members of the Federation)

#### Thornwick (Primary Setting)
- **Location**: Central Aldara, on the River Thorn
- **Population**: ~85,000 (in Varn's lifetime)
- **Character**: Industrial center, known for metalwork and engineering
- **Key Districts**:
  - The Copperage: Artisan quarter, Varn's childhood home
  - Millward: Industrial district along the river
  - Guildrow: Administrative center with guild halls
  - Ashford Heights: Wealthy residential area
  - The Narrows: Working-class housing
- **Government**: Council of Twelve (elected by guild members)
- **Notable Buildings**:
  - The Guildhall (central administration)
  - Thornwick Pressworks (Varn's printing facility)
  - The Academy of Practical Sciences
  - St. Aldric's Cathedral
  - The Irongate (main city entrance)

#### Hartwell (Rival City)
- **Location**: 120 miles northeast of Thornwick
- **Population**: ~70,000
- **Character**: Commercial hub, banking center, rival to Thornwick
- **Known For**: Hartwell Guild of Mechanists (rivals to Thornwick engineers)
- **Key Figure**: Edmund Blackwood (Varn's rival)

#### Keswick
- **Location**: Southern coast
- **Population**: ~40,000
- **Character**: Port city, center of maritime trade
- **Known For**: Shipbuilding, foreign goods, the Southern Workshops

#### Marston
- **Location**: Southwest, inland
- **Population**: ~30,000
- **Character**: Mining center, iron and coal
- **Known For**: Raw materials, the Marston Forges

#### Dunmore
- **Location**: Far south, coastal
- **Population**: ~25,000
- **Character**: Academic city, oldest university
- **Known For**: Dunmore University, theoretical sciences

#### Northgate
- **Location**: Northern frontier
- **Population**: ~15,000
- **Character**: Military outpost, frontier trade
- **Known For**: Wool, timber, border defense

### Geographic Features

- **River Thorn**: Major waterway through Thornwick, powers mills
- **The Coalhills**: Range east of Thornwick, coal and iron deposits
- **Aldaran Plain**: Central agricultural region
- **The Seaward Mountains**: Southern coastal range
- **Bridgewater Bay**: Harbor serving Keswick
- **The Northern Reaches**: Sparsely populated frontier beyond Northgate

## Characters

### Varn Family

#### Elias Varn (1738-1821)
- **Protagonist and narrator**
- **Physical**: Tall, lean, with prominent nose and keen grey eyes. In later years, white hair and slight stoop.
- **Personality**: Curious, persistent, occasionally stubborn. Values empirical observation and practical application. Dry wit.
- **Speech pattern**: Precise, occasionally technical, fond of mechanical metaphors.

#### Aldric Varn (1705-1768)
- **Elias's father**
- **Occupation**: Journeyman coppersmith
- **Character**: Hardworking, practical, limited ambition. Died of lung ailment from metalwork.
- **Legacy**: Taught Elias basic metalworking, encouraged curiosity.

#### Maren Varn née Thornley (1712-1790)
- **Elias's mother**
- **Character**: Clever, literate (unusual for her class), arranged Elias's education.
- **Background**: Daughter of a failed merchant, educated above her station.

#### Helena Varn née Wren (1742-1800)
- **Elias's wife**
- **Marriage**: Wed in 1761, died in 1800
- **Character**: Practical, supportive, managed household and business accounts. Strong-willed.
- **Background**: Daughter of a prosperous textile merchant.

#### Thomas Varn (1762-1835)
- **Elias's son**
- **Character**: Capable but lacks father's genius; continues foundry business
- **Role**: Inherits Varn & Colter Foundry

#### Margaret Varn (1773-1850)
- **Elias's daughter**
- **Character**: Intellectually gifted; marries scholar; involved in Academy
- **Marriage**: Weds Professor Aldric Crane in 1795

### Mentors and Colleagues

#### Master Doran Hask (1698-1772)
- **Role**: Elias's master during apprenticeship
- **Occupation**: Master clockmaker, owner of Hask's Horologium
- **Character**: Exacting, traditional, but recognized Elias's talent. Widower.
- **Physical**: Short, barrel-chested, missing two fingers on left hand (workshop accident).
- **Legacy**: Taught precision craftsmanship; died during Great Fire.

#### Silas Colter (1735-1810)
- **Role**: Business partner, co-founder of Varn & Colter Foundry
- **Background**: Metalworker's son, excellent business sense
- **Character**: Gregarious, practical, handled commercial matters
- **Relationship**: Lifelong friend; balanced Elias's technical focus

#### Mother Greave (1690-1758)
- **Role**: Elias's first teacher
- **Real Name**: Annabel Greave
- **Background**: Widow of a schoolmaster, ran informal school
- **Character**: Patient, encouraged reading and curiosity

#### Professor Aldric Crane (1765-1840)
- **Role**: Son-in-law, Academy colleague
- **Occupation**: Natural philosopher, first Chair of Theoretical Mechanics
- **Character**: Brilliant theorist, complements Elias's practical approach

### Rivals and Antagonists

#### Edmund Blackwood (1740-1808)
- **Role**: Primary rival, Hartwell engineer
- **Title**: Master of the Hartwell Guild of Mechanists
- **Character**: Brilliant but unscrupulous, claims credit for others' work
- **Conflicts**: Patent disputes, railway competition, trade war involvement
- **Physical**: Heavyset, ruddy complexion, ostentatious dress

#### Alderman Jasper Stokes (1730-1798)
- **Role**: Political antagonist on Thornwick Council
- **Background**: Wealthy merchant, opposes industrialization
- **Character**: Conservative, protects guild monopolies
- **Motivation**: Fears machines will displace traditional craftsmen

#### Lord Halford Wrenley (1720-1793)
- **Role**: Aristocratic skeptic
- **Background**: Largest landowner near Thornwick
- **Character**: Views engineers as "mere mechanics," opposes railway through his lands

### Other Significant Characters

#### Jonas Petworth (1745-1812)
- **Role**: Colleague, fellow inventor
- **Specialty**: Hydraulics and water systems
- **Character**: Quiet, methodical, Varn's closest technical collaborator

#### Captain Rufus Drake (1738-1796)
- **Role**: Military engineer, ally during Defense of Thornwick
- **Background**: Federation Army, engineering corps
- **Character**: Disciplined, pragmatic, respects Varn's expertise

#### Willem Hartley (1755-1825)
- **Role**: Younger protégé
- **Background**: Orphan, educated by Academy
- **Character**: Talented, eventually succeeds Varn as Academy head

#### Alderman Marcus Wren (1715-1788)
- **Role**: Father-in-law
- **Occupation**: Textile merchant
- **Character**: Initially skeptical of Elias, later supportive
- **Significance**: Funded early ventures

## Organizations

### Guilds

#### The Clockmakers' Guild of Thornwick
- **Role**: Traditional craft guild, controls horology trade
- **Structure**: Masters, journeymen, apprentices
- **Politics**: Conservative, resistant to new methods
- **Headquarters**: Guildrow, the Clockmakers' Hall

#### The Mechanists' Guild of Thornwick
- **Founded**: 1770, partly due to Varn's advocacy
- **Role**: Represents new industrial engineers
- **Difference from Clockmakers**: Embraces steam power, large-scale manufacturing

#### The Hartwell Guild of Mechanists
- **Role**: Rival organization in Hartwell
- **Leader**: Edmund Blackwood
- **Character**: More commercially aggressive, less collaborative

### Institutions

#### The Thornwick Steam Collective
- **Founded**: 1768 by Elias Varn
- **Purpose**: Informal society for discussing steam power advances
- **Membership**: Engineers, natural philosophers, interested merchants
- **Meetings**: Monthly at the Brass Wheel tavern, later Academy

#### The Academy of Practical Sciences
- **Founded**: 1790 by Elias Varn
- **Purpose**: Formal education in engineering and applied science
- **Location**: Former Wrenley estate, donated after Lord Wrenley's death
- **Structure**: Lectures, workshops, library, laboratory
- **First Chairs**:
  - Mechanics: Elias Varn
  - Theoretical Mechanics: Aldric Crane
  - Hydraulics: Jonas Petworth

#### The Federation Council
- **Role**: Governs relations between city-states
- **Location**: Rotates between cities
- **Powers**: Trade agreements, mutual defense, currency standards

## Technology

### Established Technologies (Pre-Varn Era)

- **Clockwork**: Precision gears, escapements, spring mechanisms
- **Water mills**: Power for grinding, early manufacturing
- **Basic steam**: Newcomen-style atmospheric engines for mine drainage
- **Metallurgy**: Iron smelting, copper and brass working
- **Printing**: Hand-operated presses

### Varn's Innovations

#### The Repeating Escapement (1763)
- **Purpose**: Self-correcting clock mechanism
- **Significance**: First major patent, established reputation
- **Principle**: Secondary escapement corrects drift in primary

#### The Pressworks Engine (1770)
- **Purpose**: Steam-powered printing press
- **Significance**: First practical industrial application of steam in Thornwick
- **Output**: 500 sheets per hour vs. 50 by hand

#### The Varn Governor (1778)
- **Purpose**: Pressure regulation for steam engines
- **Significance**: Major safety and efficiency improvement
- **Principle**: Centrifugal feedback mechanism controls steam flow

#### The Railway Engine (1783)
- **Purpose**: Steam locomotion on rails
- **Significance**: Enabled Northern Railway
- **Note**: Collaborative development with Jonas Petworth

### Industrial Terminology

- **Presswork**: Steam-powered machinery
- **Governor**: Pressure/speed regulation device
- **Escapement**: Clock mechanism controlling gear movement
- **Flywheel**: Rotating mass for energy storage
- **Condenser**: Steam-to-water conversion for engine efficiency
- **Gauge**: Pressure measurement device
- **Boiler**: Steam generation vessel
- **Piston**: Moving component in steam cylinder

## Economy and Society

### Currency

- **Mark**: Primary unit (silver coin)
- **Penny**: 1/100th of a Mark (copper coin)
- **Crown**: 10 Marks (gold coin, rare)
- **Common prices** (circa 1770):
  - Skilled worker's daily wage: 2-3 Marks
  - Loaf of bread: 3 pennies
  - Fine clock: 50-200 Marks
  - Steam engine: 2,000-10,000 Marks

### Social Structure

- **Nobility**: Landowners (Lords, like Wrenley), limited power in city-states
- **Merchants**: Wealthy trading class, significant political power
- **Guild Masters**: Skilled craftsmen, control trades
- **Journeymen**: Qualified craftsmen, employed by masters
- **Apprentices**: Trainees, bound to masters (typically 5-7 years)
- **Laborers**: Unskilled workers, factory hands

## Language and Style Notes

### Forms of Address

- **Master**: Qualified guild member (Master Hask, Master Varn)
- **Alderman**: City council member
- **Lord/Lady**: Nobility
- **Professor**: Academy instructor
- **Captain/Colonel**: Military ranks

### Common Expressions

- "By the Federation" - mild oath
- "Thorn and river" - Thornwick-specific oath
- "Worth his marks" - valuable person
- "Running on steam" - working hard/efficiently
- "Gears in alignment" - things going well
- "Slipped a cog" - made a mistake

### Narrative Voice

Elias writes in first person, retrospective. He is:
- Precise and observational
- Occasionally self-deprecating
- Fond of technical digressions
- Philosophical about progress
- Sometimes critical of younger self

Example tone: "I confess that my early confidence exceeded my competence, as the young are wont to believe themselves inventors of principles which wiser men had long since established."
